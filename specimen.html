Webfont specimen — v0.3 Task 4

Two faces, the same words: the current system-serif stack and a vendored EB Garamond. Print this (each face lands on its own A4 sheet), lay the two sheets beside each other, and judge the letter body and the tighter booklet body on paper. Either outcome is fine; record the call and the evidence in docs/decisions.md.

To activate the EB column, drop EBGaramond-Regular.ttf (and EBGaramond-Italic.ttf for the italics) into ./fonts/ alongside the other OFL faces, with the licence file. No webfont, no network — a local file only.

Open when you can't sleep

Dear friend,

I am writing this on an ordinary Tuesday, the kind you forget by Thursday, because I wanted you to have something in my hand for a day I cannot be there. The kettle is on. The afternoon light is laying itself flat across the table where we first sat.

There is nothing urgent here. I only wanted to say that you were loved plainly, and for a long time, and that whatever the year is when you find this (2126, for all I know), a few things will not have changed. Kindness still costs nothing. A good loaf is still worth the wait, and a letter is still a fine way to offer one.

Keep well, and write to someone. With love.
