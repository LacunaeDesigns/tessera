About Tessera

Tessera is a writing room for letters that open years from now. Each sealed letter is an ordinary folder of plain files and a small print kit. The paper is the letter; the folder is its convenience copy.

The format is public domain and documented inside every letter, so a folder found in forty years explains itself. The tool is a single page that works offline, from a USB stick if it must.

The honest part

Tessera cannot stop anyone opening a letter early, and will not pretend it can. Sealing here is ritual and custody, not enforcement. There are no delivery promises, because there is no deliverer. What holds instead: a named keeper, a printed date, and the small ceremony of a token broken in two.

Your letters never leave this device. There is no account and no analytics of any kind.
