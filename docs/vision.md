# Vision

## The sentence (author's words, canon — 2026-07-10)

> "A place where people and I can store the things that we can send beyond time in the future — for those who could use a hand and in need of guidance, and also something to look forward to."

Everything in Tessera answers to the three promises inside that sentence.

## The three promises

**1. Beyond time.** A letter sealed today must be openable in forty years without any company, server, format, or person — including the author of this project — having survived. This is why paper is the reference copy, the format is public domain, the tool is a dependency-free static page, and no feature may introduce a moving part that has to stay alive. The test is named in the spec: the *century test*.

**2. A hand extended.** Some letters are not for ceremonies; they are for the worst day. "Open when you doubt yourself." "Open when it's 3 a.m. and you can't sleep." A letter from someone who loved you, waiting patiently in a drawer for exactly that night, is a form of help no app notification will ever be. This audience — the future reader who needs guidance and kindness — is first-class, which is why the *open-when* prompt set ships in v0.1, not later.

**3. Something to look forward to.** A person with sealed letters in their future is a person with appointments to keep with hope. The registry's *letter ladder* makes this visible: your own correspondence with time, stretching ahead of you. Writing the letters is the other half of the gift — articulating, today, what you want a future person to have.

## Who it serves

- **Parents and partners** — letters to a child at every milestone; to each other at anniversaries not yet reached.
- **The person someone will be on a hard day** — the open-when drawer.
- **Future selves** — yourself at 40, 60, 80; yourself on the day you retire, or the day you doubt the path.
- **Strangers of the far future** — the letters addressed "to whoever finds this," which the commons (v1.0) and the Hundred-Year Room (v2.x) exist for.
- **The people in between** — custodians. Tessera treats carrying a letter forward as an honoured role with named promises, not an afterthought.

## The lineage

Perpetūra — the author's interactive fiction — is five stories across five centuries, secretly connected by one feeling that survives them all. Tessera is that premise made practical: not fiction about a feeling crossing time, but the instrument by which real ones do. The two projects share a soul and almost nothing else — which is the point.

## What success looks like

Not scale. Success is: the format is simple enough that someone reimplements it without asking permission; a letter sealed in 2026 is opened in 2066 and everything still works, including the emotion; teachers and wedding guests use the kits without ever learning who made them; and the author's own founding letters are waiting, sealed, in a drawer at home.
