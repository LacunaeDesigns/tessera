# Positioning — prior art, the gap, the name

*Based on a web scan run 2026-07-10 (search-engine evidence, not an authoritative trademark search). Sources at the end.*

## The landscape

**Email-your-future-self services** — FutureMe.org (since 2002), Sealed, The Online Time Capsule, TimeLock, time-capsule.io and kin. All: account + server + scheduled delivery. Every one depends on the company surviving until your open date; none publishes a format. FutureMe's longevity is the product — which is exactly the promise Tessera refuses to make.

**Blockchain time-capsules and dead-man's-switches** — TimeDAO, Sarcophagus, EstateVault, killcord; commercial SaaS variants (Cipherwill, LastSend). These have unlock mechanics and even "tokens," but require wallets, gas, and a live protocol — a different, heavier dependency, not an escape from dependency. No paper, no plain files.

**Paper keepsake products** — *Letters to My Baby* (Chronicle Books, 2M+ copies) and the whole Etsy "open when" kit market. The ritual is real and beloved — and there is no format, no digital layer, no verification, no custody guidance. These prove the demand; they don't serve the century.

**Archival nonprofits** — Permanent.org ("forever formats," hosted), the International Time Capsule Society (a registry). Mission-locked but still services.

**Long-term-thinking ethos** — the Long Now Foundation (10,000 Year Clock, Long Bets, the Rosetta Project's nickel language disc). The right stance at civilisational scale; Tessera borrows the stance at domestic scale — a drawer, not a mountain.

**Nearest name-neighbour** — *Tessera Memories* (Little Lumen Labs, iOS): a private memory-journal app with a plain-Markdown-export philosophy. Same name, adjacent niche, but: not letters-to-the-future, not paper-first, not an open spec, no token, iCloud-bound. **Copy rule: never describe Tessera as a "journal" or "memories" app** — ours is correspondence across time, and the distinction is also the differentiation.

## The gap (verified)

No found project combines **all four legs**: (1) an open, public-domain file-format spec; (2) a no-account, no-server, one-letter-one-folder local model; (3) a print-ready paper ritual as the reference copy; (4) a deterministic two-half reunification token. Every close analog is missing at least one — usually the format, always the token. That combination is the pitch, and it held up to the scan.

## The name

**Tessera** — after the *tessera hospitalis*: a token broken in two between host and guest, halves kept across generations, reunited to be recognised. The scan found many "Tessera" businesses (semiconductor IP, SaaS, biotech, a ConsenSys blockchain component, dev agencies) spread across unrelated trademark classes, one already-cancelled software-adjacent US mark, and **no dominant owner in the letters/stationery/free-software space. Verdict: hard legal conflict unlikely.**

Standing cautions (author gates before anything commercial):
1. Run a real USPTO/EUIPO clearance in Class 9 (software) and Class 16 (paper goods) — the web scan is not a legal search.
2. Differentiate from *Tessera Memories* in all public copy (rule above).
3. GitHub is crowded with unrelated "tessera" repos — discoverability copy should always say "Tessera letters" or "Tessera format."

**Fallback considered and rejected:** *Symbolon* — a 30-year-old tarot/oracle deck brand (AGM Müller) plus a dating app already own the word in thematically adjacent ritual space. Worse than Tessera, not better.
