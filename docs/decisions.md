- **2026-07-11 · Hardware keys interrupt the greeting; interruption means it's the writer's turn.** Typing on a real keyboard while the desk auto-types the greeting (date line + "For …,") interleaved the two streams — the typer interval kept appending greeting characters after each user keystroke, and the garbled text could be sealed into `letter.txt` (reproduced: "11 JXul 2026…"). - **2026-07-11 · `js/crypt.js` is the one module exempt from the no-randomness rule.** The pure-module contract (CLAUDE.md) forbids `Math.random`, `Date`, and locale calls so modules stay deterministic. Encryption cannot be deterministic and safe: a fixed salt or IV would defeat AES-GCM and PBKDF2. So `crypt.js` draws a fresh random salt (16 bytes) and IV (12 bytes) per seal via `crypto.getRandomValues` — the only sanctioned randomness source (never `Math.random`), and it still takes no `Date` or locale. To keep it fixture-testable, `encryptLetter(text, pass, opts)` accepts injected `salt`/`iv`/`iterations`; `tools/test-crypt.js` uses fixed values for byte-exact assertions and separately checks that two real seals (no injection) produce unique salt and IV. The forbidden-source scan in that suite enforces no `Math.random`/`Date` in the file.

- **2026-07-11 · The envelope is genuinely no-cut: glue the two overlapping side edges, not separate tabs.** The author's first physical print (Gate 4) caught that the envelope template promised "No cutting required" while drawing two separate glue-tab boxes, and a tab can only fold independently if it is cut free on its sides, so the promise and the picture disagreed.
